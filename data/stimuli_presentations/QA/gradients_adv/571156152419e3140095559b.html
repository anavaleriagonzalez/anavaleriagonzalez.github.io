Question:

how was the efficiency of a steam engine typically evaluated ?

Context:

the historical measure of a steam engine ' s energy efficiency was its " duty " . the concept of duty was first introduced by watt in order to illustrate how much more efficient his engines were over the earlier new com en designs . duty is the number of foot - pounds of work delivered by burning one bush el ( 94 pounds ) of coal . the best examples of new com en designs had a duty of about 7 million , but most were closer to 5 million . watt ' s original low - pressure designs were able to deliver duty as high as 25 million , but averaged about 17 . this was a three - fold improvement over the average new com en design . early watt engines equipped with high - pressure steam improved this to 65 million .
